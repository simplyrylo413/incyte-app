History pill — 3 design options

Each option preserves: status (saved/done), date, what was trained, total volume, set count, and a resume/view action. All three keep the same surface treatment as Today's movement pills so the visual language stays consistent across views.

A

Single-line minimal

All info on one row: status chip · date · movements summary · total volume · chevron. Whole pill is tappable; no separate Resume button. Best for at-a-glance scanning of many history rows.

Saved Wed · May 13 Y raises · Dips · Dips +2 1,710lb
Done Tue · May 12 Bench · Squat · Lat Pulldown +5 3,420lb
Done Mon · May 11 Bench · Incline DB · Lat Pulldown 2,860lb
Character Most compressed. Reads like a list of dated receipts. Tradeoff: long movement names get truncated; set count is omitted (folded into the volume figure). Best when you scan history mostly to find a date.
B

Two-line metric strip

Top row: status + date + time. Bottom row: volume / movements / sets in mono numerics. No separate Resume button — tap the whole pill. Slightly taller than A but exposes more stats per row.

Saved Wed · May 13 2:01 PM
1,710lb · 5 movements · 2 sets
Done Tue · May 12 7:42 PM
3,420lb · 8 movements · 22 sets · RPE 7.8
Done Mon · May 11 6:15 PM
2,860lb · 6 movements · 18 sets · RPE 7.5
Character Densest information per row without feeling crowded. Bottom-row pattern (volume + meta) reads like a stat sheet. Best when the user actually reads history to compare sessions. Tradeoff: more vertical real estate than A.
C

Ledger (stat-first)

Stat leads: total volume in big mono numerals on the left, context (movements · sets · RPE) below it. Right side: date, top-right. Left-edge accent bar — teal for Done, slate for Saved.

1,710lb 5 mv · 2 sets
Wed · May 13 Saved
3,420lb 8 mv · 22 sets · RPE 7.8
Tue · May 12 Done
2,860lb 6 mv · 18 sets · RPE 7.5
Mon · May 11 Done
Character The volume number is the focal point. Treats history rows like ledger entries — the lifter's progress over time is the story. Tradeoff: movement names are gone (you'd see them by tapping in). Best when volume and intensity are what you actually compare across sessions.
